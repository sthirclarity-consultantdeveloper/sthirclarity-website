Three things that block high performers

After working with professionals across consulting, business, and leadership — the block almost always comes from one of three places. Not intelligence. Not preparation. Something more structural.

01
Cognitive Overload

Too many competing elements in working memory at once. When the mind is managing too much, the signal gets lost in the noise. Clarity breaks before the words do.

02
Self-Monitoring Interference

Running a parallel evaluation of how you're coming across — while you're trying to perform. The second track steals bandwidth from the first. The result looks like hesitation.

03
Unautomated First Principles

Frameworks that are intellectually understood but not yet internalised. Under pressure, you revert to what's automatic — not what you know. The gap shows up exactly when you need it least.
